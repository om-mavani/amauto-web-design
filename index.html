  • Gasoline is the most common type of fuel used in a vintage car. It's usually the cheapest option and it's also readily available. However, it does have a few drawbacks. Gasoline evaporates quickly and leaves residue on the engine which can cause corrosion over time. Diesel was once a popular choice for fuel, but has become less common in recent years due to its higher cost and environmental impact. Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) is an alternative that can be used in certain vintage cars with LPG-compatible engines. It is usually more expensive than gasoline or diesel, but it doesn't leave residue on the engine because it's vaporized before combustion.
